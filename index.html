The Chameleon

Chameleons are a family, Chamaeleonidae , of small scaly sauropsids (reptiles).
There are about 161 species of chameleons , most of them in Africa south of the Sahara.
In America, lizards of the anole family, which bear no special relationship to true chameleons , are often called chameleons.
They are famous for their ability to change color depending on the circumstances, for their fast and elongated tongue, and for their eyes, which can be moved independently of each other.
